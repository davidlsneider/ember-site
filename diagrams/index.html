EMBER · THE THREE DIAGRAMS

D1 — Custody, before and after

AUDIENCE: THE VENDOR CEO · OWNS: THE INVERSION

The vendor serves the entire product and can read none of it.

D2 — One session, precisely

AUDIENCE: THE VENDOR'S ENGINEERS · OWNS: THE RUNTIME PATH

The whole session happens inside the vendor's product. The content is readable in exactly two places — the app on the user's device, and the attested room — and Ember is the separate, verifiable service embedded in it.

D3 — The dial

AUDIENCE: THE END CUSTOMER AND THEIR AUDITOR · OWNS: TWO POSITIONS, ONE INVARIANT

One room, two homes, one root — accounts controlled by the owners' keys decide what may run. Memory moves the same way. This is how governance gets into people's hands.
