What does their security team actually get?

Attestation that the audited build is what actually ran, plus a receipt for every session. Both are checkable without asking you or us, and both can be forwarded straight into the review thread.

Is this a crypto thing?

No token, nothing to trade. Receipts anchor to a public ledger for one reason: it’s the only log a vendor can’t quietly edit. Your customer’s key is a passkey, one tap.

How does it actually work?

Your application logic runs content-pinned in a sealed environment: the hardware proves it’s running the exact build that was audited. Your customer’s data enters encrypted under a key only they hold, decrypts only inside that environment, and nothing persists after the session. Inference is invoked from within it, so content never passes through your servers. Every session writes a signed receipt (what ran, where, under whose key) to a ledger nobody, including us, can edit. The cryptographic core is a blockchain-attested TEE, in production today.
