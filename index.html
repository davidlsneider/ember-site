NOTES

M-of-N quorum. Policy changes require M-of-N signatures from keys your team already holds — YubiKeys, passkeys, wallets. No admin can act alone; no vendor can act at all.

The chain. Any EVM chain. Authority root, policy, and receipts live there — they outlast every vendor, including us.

The governance node. A chain-attested TEE: an $8,000 appliance on-prem, or hosted by us. It enforces policy and holds nothing you can't reproduce — if there's a fire, save something else.

The private GPU API. Venues present attestation — hardware, stack, and location — before payment releases. Today: the US, Netherlands, Germany, India, and Japan (pilot); the map grows. Your racks and the frontier APIs sit behind the same API.

Settlement. Stablecoins fund every venue through the node; payment moves only on policy. Treasury can live directly in the system — keys sealed in the node — or behind a qualified custodian you plug in. Same policy either way.

The record. A receipt for every allow and every refusal, anchored to the chain.

THE POINT

Everything your company runs on is rented — the models, the logins, the payments. Renting is fine. Having no way out is not.

Ember puts your authority — keys, budgets, rules, receipts — on an EVM chain you choose, enforced by a chain-attested TEE in your office or hosted by us.

Your cloud IAM stays. It keeps answering who; Ember answers how much, where, and with whose keys.

Route everything through it on day one. Move anything to owned — private GPUs first — one service at a time. Rent what you like; own what you must; switch without asking.

It holds nothing precious. If there's a fire, save something else — the root of authority is the chain.
